# Executive Security Brief

## Situation

Northstar Mission Services relies on cloud services, remote users, partner access, and sensitive information. Traditional security based mainly on network location is no longer enough.

The main concern is not whether an attack will be attempted. The concern is whether the organization can prevent a small security issue from becoming a major business disruption.

## Recommended Direction

Adopt a Zero Trust architecture and manage it through the seven-step Risk Management Framework.

The approach is simple:

1. Check every access request.
2. Limit access to the minimum required.
3. Assume an attacker may gain access.
4. Detect unusual activity quickly.
5. Contain incidents before they spread.
6. Measure whether protections are working.
7. Give leaders clear risk information.

## Top Business Risks

| Priority | Risk | Business Impact |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Administrator account compromise | Loss of control over cloud resources and sensitive systems |
| 2 | Access from an unmanaged or compromised endpoint | Malware spread, data loss, and unauthorized access |
| 3 | Public cloud misconfiguration | Exposure of sensitive information |
| 4 | Missing security logs | Delayed detection and incomplete investigations |
| 5 | Unresolved critical vulnerabilities | Increased chance of successful exploitation |
| 6 | Insider or third-party misuse | Data leakage, fraud, or compliance failure |

## Proposed Security Model

### Identity First

Require strong authentication, separate administrator accounts, and time-limited privileged access.

### Trusted Endpoints

Require managed and compliant endpoints for sensitive access.

### Protected Data

Classify sensitive information, encrypt it, and monitor high-risk movement.

### Smaller Blast Radius

Segment workloads and restrict communication to approved paths.

### Central Monitoring

Combine identity, endpoint, cloud, application, and data signals in one monitoring process.

### Continuous Governance

Use policy, evidence, metrics, and risk reviews to prevent security controls from weakening over time.

## Expected Business Benefits

- Lower probability of major account compromise
- Faster detection and containment
- Reduced risk of data exposure
- Improved mission and service resilience
- Clearer ownership of cyber risk
- Better audit and inspection readiness
- More informed investment decisions

## Leadership Decisions Required

1. Approve Zero Trust as the security direction.
2. Approve identity and privileged access as the first priority.
3. Require all high-risk findings to have an owner and target date.
4. Require approved exceptions to include compensating controls and expiration dates.
5. Review cyber risk and roadmap progress quarterly.

## Success Measures

| Measure | Target |
|---|---:|
| Privileged accounts using strong authentication | 100% |
| Privileged roles using time-limited activation | At least 95% |
| Managed endpoints reporting compliant | At least 95% |
| Critical log sources connected and healthy | 100% |
| Critical vulnerabilities past due | 0 |
| Unauthorized public exposure of critical resources | 0 |
| High-risk exceptions past expiration | 0 |

## Executive Summary

This plan does not attempt to remove all risk. It creates a practical way to reduce risk, identify remaining weaknesses, and give leaders enough information to make responsible decisions.
