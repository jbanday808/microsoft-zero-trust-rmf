# 180-Day Security Roadmap

## Roadmap Goal

Reduce the highest business risks first, establish measurable control coverage, and give leaders clear decision points.

## Phase 1 — Governance and Visibility: Days 0–30

### Actions

1. Approve system scope, ownership, and Zero Trust policy.
2. Confirm the top eight risks.
3. Assign control and evidence owners.
4. Connect critical identity, endpoint, cloud, and application logs.
5. Enable cloud posture monitoring.
6. Apply baseline cloud policies in audit mode.
7. Establish monthly technical and quarterly executive reporting.

### Business Outcome

Leaders gain a reliable view of current risk and responsibility.

### Decision Gate

Confirm that every critical asset and high-risk finding has an owner.

## Phase 2 — Identity and Endpoint Protection: Days 31–60

### Actions

1. Inventory privileged accounts and roles.
2. Separate standard and administrator accounts.
3. Require strong multifactor authentication.
4. Configure time-limited privileged access.
5. Establish emergency access accounts.
6. Enroll corporate endpoints.
7. Deploy endpoint detection and response.
8. Require compliant endpoints for sensitive access.

### Business Outcome

A stolen password or unmanaged endpoint is less likely to cause a major breach.

### Decision Gate

Confirm that privileged-access and endpoint targets are being met.

## Phase 3 — Workload and Data Protection: Days 61–90

### Actions

1. Replace stored credentials with managed identities.
2. Protect secrets and certificates in Key Vault.
3. Review application roles and API permissions.
4. Approve data classifications.
5. Apply labels and DLP to priority data.
6. Validate encryption.
7. Remove unauthorized public exposure.
8. Define network trust zones.

### Business Outcome

Sensitive applications and data receive consistent protection.

### Decision Gate

Confirm that all critical applications and sensitive data stores have named owners.

## Phase 4 — Validation and Improvement: Days 91–180

### Actions

1. Test approved and blocked access.
2. Test endpoint isolation and session revocation.
3. Conduct a credential-compromise exercise.
4. Test backup restoration and emergency access.
5. Move proven cloud policies from audit to enforcement.
6. Tune security alerts.
7. Automate recurring evidence collection.
8. Review risk and metrics monthly.
9. Produce quarterly executive risk reports.

### Business Outcome

The organization can demonstrate that controls work and that risk is improving.

### Decision Gate

Decide whether remaining risk is acceptable and approve the next maturity phase.

## Key Targets

| Target | Goal |
|---|---:|
| Privileged accounts using strong authentication | 100% |
| Privileged roles using time-limited activation | At least 95% |
| Managed endpoints reporting compliant | At least 95% |
| Critical log sources connected and healthy | 100% |
| Critical vulnerabilities past due | 0 |
| Unauthorized public exposure of critical resources | 0 |
| High-risk exceptions past expiration | 0 |

## Key Dependencies

- Executive sponsorship
- Identity and endpoint licensing
- Named application and data owners
- Platform-team capacity
- SOC monitoring capacity
- Approved exception and escalation process
